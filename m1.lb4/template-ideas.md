# Document Template Ideas

## Overview

This document contains 30+ template ideas organised by function. Use this as inspiration for building your own template library. You don't need all of these - start with the 3-5 documents you create most frequently.

---

## Meeting & Communication Templates

### 1. Meeting Notes
**Use when:** Documenting any meeting
**Key sections:** Details, Attendees, Agenda, Discussion, Decisions, Actions, Next Steps
**Priority:** HIGH - most people create these multiple times per week

### 2. Meeting Agenda
**Use when:** Planning a meeting
**Key sections:** Objectives, Topics (with time allocations), Prep Required, Participants
**Priority:** Medium - improves meeting effectiveness

### 3. One-to-One Record
**Use when:** Manager/report check-ins
**Key sections:** What's going well, Challenges, Career development, Action items, Personal notes
**Priority:** Medium - creates continuity across check-ins

### 4. Standup Notes
**Use when:** Daily team standups
**Key sections:** Date, Attendees, Yesterday, Today, Blockers (per person)
**Priority:** Low - often handled by other tools

### 5. Workshop Facilitation Guide
**Use when:** Running workshops or collaborative sessions
**Key sections:** Objectives, Participants, Agenda, Materials needed, Activities, Expected outputs
**Priority:** Low - depends on role

---

## Project Management Templates

### 6. Project Brief
**Use when:** Proposing or initiating a project
**Key sections:** Background, Objectives, Scope, Success criteria, Timeline, Resources, Risks
**Priority:** HIGH - essential for project clarity

### 7. Weekly Status Update
**Use when:** Reporting project progress
**Key sections:** Week of, Accomplishments, In Progress, Blockers, Next Week, Help Needed
**Priority:** HIGH - keeps stakeholders informed

### 8. Project Retrospective
**Use when:** Ending a project or phase
**Key sections:** What went well, What could improve, Key learnings, Recommendations
**Priority:** Medium - drives continuous improvement

### 9. Decision Log
**Use when:** Tracking important decisions
**Key sections:** Date, Decision, Context, Alternatives considered, Rationale, Owner
**Priority:** Medium - valuable for complex projects

### 10. Risk Register
**Use when:** Managing project risks
**Key sections:** Risk description, Likelihood, Impact, Mitigation, Owner, Status
**Priority:** Low - depends on project complexity

### 11. Change Request
**Use when:** Proposing project scope changes
**Key sections:** Description, Reason, Impact, Alternatives, Recommendation, Approval
**Priority:** Low - depends on formality needs

---

## Client Work Templates

### 12. Client Onboarding Checklist
**Use when:** Setting up new clients
**Key sections:** Client info, Pre-onboarding tasks, Week 1 tasks, Ongoing setup, Notes
**Priority:** HIGH - ensures consistent client experience

### 13. Discovery Call Notes
**Use when:** Initial client conversations
**Key sections:** Client background, Current challenges, Goals, Budget/timeline, Next steps
**Priority:** HIGH - captures crucial early information

### 14. Client Progress Report
**Use when:** Regular client updates
**Key sections:** Period covered, Progress summary, Deliverables, Issues, Upcoming work
**Priority:** HIGH - maintains client relationships

### 15. Feedback Request
**Use when:** Gathering testimonials or reviews
**Key sections:** Greeting, Specific questions, Response format, Deadline, Thank you
**Priority:** Medium - builds social proof

### 16. Project Handover
**Use when:** Transitioning work between team members
**Key sections:** Overview, Current status, Key contacts, Open items, Files/access, Notes
**Priority:** Medium - ensures continuity

### 17. Client Offboarding Checklist
**Use when:** Ending client relationships
**Key sections:** Final deliverables, Access revocation, Archive tasks, Feedback, Referral request
**Priority:** Low - less frequent but important

## Content Creation Templates

### 18. Blog Post Outline
**Use when:** Planning blog content
**Key sections:** Title, Target audience, Key message, Outline, Call to action, SEO keywords
**Priority:** Medium - depends on content volume

### 19. Case Study Structure
**Use when:** Creating client success stories
**Key sections:** Client background, Challenge, Solution, Results, Testimonial
**Priority:** Medium - powerful for marketing

### 20. Report Framework
**Use when:** Creating formal reports
**Key sections:** Executive summary, Background, Methodology, Findings, Recommendations
**Priority:** Medium - depends on role

### 21. Proposal Template
**Use when:** Pitching to clients or stakeholders
**Key sections:** Context, Proposed solution, Approach, Timeline, Investment, Next steps
**Priority:** HIGH for sales roles - directly impacts revenue

### 22. Email Template (Complex)
**Use when:** Recurring complex emails
**Key sections:** Subject line options, Opening, Body sections, Call to action, Signature
**Priority:** Low - simple emails don't need templates

## Operations Templates

### 31. Process Documentation
**Use when:** Documenting how to do something
**Key sections:** Purpose, Prerequisites, Steps, Troubleshooting, Related processes
**Priority:** Medium - builds organisational knowledge

### 32. Incident Report
**Use when:** Documenting problems or failures
**Key sections:** Summary, Timeline, Impact, Root cause, Resolution, Prevention
**Priority:** Low - hopefully infrequent

### 33. Request for Proposal (RFP)
**Use when:** Soliciting vendor proposals
**Key sections:** Company background, Requirements, Evaluation criteria, Timeline, Submission

## Getting Started

### If you're a consultant/agency:
Start with: Meeting Notes, Client Onboarding, Project Brief, Weekly Update, Proposal

### If you're a project manager:
Start with: Meeting Notes, Project Brief, Weekly Update, Retrospective, Decision Log

### If you're a content creator:
Start with: Blog Outline, Case Study, Meeting Notes, Editorial Calendar, Feedback Request

### If you're an individual contributor:
Start with: Meeting Notes, Daily Planning, Weekly Review, One-to-One Record
