## Voice & Tone

### Brand Personality
- **Practical**: No jargon, no fluff. Clear, actionable guidance.
- **Warm**: Approachable and supportive, never condescending.
- **Scottish**: Grounded, straightforward, with quiet confidence.
- **Expert**: Knowledgeable without being academic.

### Writing Style
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences concise
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use "you" and "your" to address clients directly
- Avoid buzzwords and corporate speak

### Tone Examples

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Headlines | Confident, direct | "Scale without the chaos" |
| Body copy | Warm, helpful | "We'll work alongside your team to build systems that stick" |
| CTAs | Action-oriented | "Let's talk growth" |

---

## Visual Style

### Photography
- Real people in real Scottish business settings
- Natural lighting, warm tones
- Authentic moments over staged shots
- Diverse representation of Scottish business community

### Graphics
- Clean, minimal line illustrations
- Use brand colours consistently
- Charts and diagrams should be clear and uncluttered
- Icons: simple, line-based, consistent weight

### Layout Principles
- Generous white space
- Left-aligned text (avoid justified)
- One key idea per section
- Visual hierarchy through size and weight, not decoration

---

## Logo Usage

### Clear Space
- Minimum clear space: height of the 'G' in GrowthPath on all sides
- Never place logo on busy backgrounds

### Minimum Size
- Print: 25mm width minimum
- Digital: 120px width minimum

### Colour Variations
- Primary: Forest Green on light backgrounds
- Reversed: White on dark backgrounds
- Never alter logo colours beyond these approved versions

---

## Application Notes

When applying this brand kit:
1. Consistency is more important than creativity
2. When in doubt, use more white space
3. Forest Green is the hero colour - use it boldly
4. Gold accents should be used sparingly for emphasis
5. Every piece should feel calm, confident, and professional
