## Persona Focus: Isla Murray

**Role**: Marketing Manager
**Challenge**: Create professional podcast content without audio engineering skills or recording equipment

### Background Relevant to This Lab

At her previous agency, Isla occasionally worked with voiceover artists for client projects. She knows what professional audio sounds like. She also knows what it costs - both in money and coordination time. Voiceover artists charge by the word, require booking, need direction, and often require multiple takes.

She's been listening to podcasts for years - particularly marketing and business content. She knows the format, the pacing, the conversational style that works. She can write podcast scripts in her sleep. Writing isn't the problem.

But she's never been comfortable on the microphone herself. Her recorded attempts sound stilted, her Edinburgh accent unpredictable, her pacing rushed when nervous. "I'm a writer, not a performer," she's said more than once.

### Internal Monologue

*"I've written five solid podcast scripts. Five episodes of genuinely useful content for Scottish startups. The kind of stuff I wish someone had told us when we were starting out.*

*But every time I hit record, I freeze. Or I ramble. Or my upstairs neighbour starts hoovering. Last weekend's attempt sounded like I was recording in a tin can during a thunderstorm.*

*I keep thinking about those AI voice tools. The ones that sound almost human now. Not the robotic text-to-speech of ten years ago - proper, natural-sounding voices.*

*What if the script is all I need? What if someone else - or something else - could be the voice of Growth Signals?"*

### What They Need From You

Isla needs audio production that:
- Transforms her written scripts into professional-quality audio
- Maintains consistent voice and style across all episodes
- Eliminates the recording, re-recording, and editing cycle
- Allows rapid iteration based on feedback
- Produces genuinely broadcast-quality output

### Why This Matters

Audio is one of the highest-barrier content formats. Video is harder, but audio still requires:
- Equipment investment
- Technical skills
- Performance ability
- Post-production time

AI voice synthesis eliminates all four barriers. If you can write a script, you can produce audio. This fundamentally changes what's possible for small teams and solo creators.

The MCP pattern continues: configure server, verify connection, use natural language, iterate on results. But audio introduces new considerations - voice selection, pronunciation handling, script formatting for speech. These skills transfer to any voice-enabled AI tool.
