## 1. Background

### Professional History
Hodl spent 15 years climbing the ranks at major consulting firms, including 8 years at one of the Big Four. He started as an analyst fresh out of his MBA, worked his way up to Senior Manager, and was on the partner track when he walked away.

The breaking point came during a project for a family-owned manufacturing business in the Borders. The client needed practical help with a specific growth challenge. Instead, Hodl's firm sold them a Â£300,000 "digital transformation programme" that the business couldn't afford and didn't need. Hodl was told to "focus on the engagement, not the relationship."

Six months later, he handed in his notice with nothing lined up except an idea and some savings.

- **Various certifications:** Strategy Consulting, Change Management, Agile methodologies

### Areas of Expertise

## 3. Current Challenges

1. **Time Poverty**
   Hodl is stretched too thin. As CEO of a small company, he's involved in everything: sales, delivery, finance, operations. He knows he should delegate more but struggles to let go.

2. **Document Disasters**
   The Great Document Disaster (sending the wrong file to a client) happened on his watch. He's embarrassed and determined to fix it, but doesn't have time to implement proper systems himself.

3. **Scaling Without Losing Quality**
   GrowthPath's reputation depends on personalised, high-quality advice. Hodl worries that as they grow, they'll become the kind of faceless consultancy he left. He hasn't figured out how to scale without diluting what makes them special.

4. **Financial Pressure**
   The company is slightly loss-making, funded by Hodl's savings. He tries not to let the team see his stress about the runway, but it affects his sleep. He needs the transformation to work quickly.

5. **Wearing the CEO Hat**
   Hodl loves the client work - the strategy discussions, the problem-solving. He's less comfortable with the CEO responsibilities: HR, admin, finances. He knows he needs to step up as a leader, not just a consultant.

## 5. Communication Style

### Email Style
- **Tone:** Direct and brief
- **Length:** Short paragraphs, bullet points where possible
- **Response Time:** Fast (usually within hours, expects same from others)
- **Pet Peeve:** Long, rambling emails that bury the key point

**Typical Email Example:**
```
Subject: Quick decision needed

Team,

Need a call on the Highland Outdoor proposal by EOD.
Options:
1. Go with 15% discount as requested
2. Hold price, add extra workshop
3. Walk away

My lean: Option 2. Thoughts?

H
```

### Meeting Style
- Prefers standing meetings (literally - he has a stand-up desk)
- Starts with the conclusion, then provides context
- Gets restless in long meetings; will push for decisions
- Known to pace when thinking through complex problems

### Feedback Style
- Direct and immediate - doesn't save feedback for reviews
- Praises publicly, critiques privately
- Can be blunt; has worked on softening delivery
- Appreciates when people push back with good arguments

### Signature Phrases
- "What's the actual problem here?"
- "Let's not boil the ocean"
- "Good enough to ship"
- "The honey badger doesnae give up"
- "What would we tell a client to do?"

## 7. Working With Hodl

### Do's
- **Be direct** - He respects people who get to the point
- **Come with solutions** - Present options, not just problems
- **Push back if you disagree** - He values people who'll challenge him (respectfully)
- **Keep him informed** - No surprises; he wants to know about issues early
- **Be reliable** - If you say you'll do something, do it

### Don'ts
- **Don't waffle** - Long explanations test his patience
- **Don't hide problems** - He'd rather know about issues early than be blindsided
- **Don't be precious** - He moves fast and expects others to keep up
- **Don't take his bluntness personally** - It's just how he communicates
- **Don't mistake urgency for emergency** - Everything feels urgent to him, but learn to calibrate

### Managing Up
- Send him brief updates, not lengthy reports
- Book 15-minute slots, not hour-long meetings
- Prepare for him to challenge your thinking
- If you need a decision, give him clear options
- Catch him early in the day (he's a morning person)
